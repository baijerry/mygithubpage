IoT Hardware Product Developer

DataTellit Inc.

What I did

DataTellit is a startup company located in the Accelerator Centre incubator, in Waterloo. They are building a smart lighting product for the household IoT market, called Xlight. Alongside developement and manufacturing teams in China, the team located in Waterloo was in charge of defining the product's features and protocols, as well as the developement of the embedded control program for the light fixture's wall switch.

Working in a team of 3, I took on a variety of tasks including software design and developement, product design, and basic hardware prototyping using various sensors and RF modules. I also researched open source libraries and defined RF and JSON protocols. The software tasks included creating the embedded C++ control program on the industrial Particle P1 board, which consisted mainly of an ARM Cortex M3 MCU, a Broadcom Wi-Fi chip, and EEPROM memory. The Particle API and Particle Cloud were used extensively to provide IoT features for the product. Product design tasks involved defining use cases, extracting engineering requirements out of feature lists, and creating potential wall switch designs using human factors and UX/UI knowledge. I also assessed in the UX design of the Android app which was created as a MVP to showcase the light prototype.

Favourite Moment

Even though we didn't have to, the other coop and I decided to implement scrum style sprints for fun. A rough product backlog was created, and was divided into stories and tasks. It was very informal but helped us manage the complexity of the control program developement.
